## Ambush at Kestral Outpost
While Chris Anthemum gathered fresh water and supplies on a small floating island, the peace was shattered without warning. Six Shattered Will cultists ambushed him near the supply shacks on the far side of the island, sending him sprinting back toward the boat with the attackers hot on his heels. Three of the cultists wielded jagged crystalline axes, their masks covering their cheeks like war paint, while the other three carried rifles and wore rocket boots that launched them into the air above the fray. The Astral Covenant met them head-on — Krish drawing his sword Silence Bane and charging into the thick of it, Quinlan unleashing a devastating barrage that sent one rifleman exploding into the sky, and Vien weaving hallucinations through the minds of the aerial gunmen to leave them dazed and stumbling. Sabra took a punishing blow from a shard axe but held the line, and together the party cut through the cultists one by one until the last aerial rifleman fell and the island went quiet.
## The Aetherborn's Calculations
Before departing, Chris Anthemum knelt beside each fallen cultist and placed two coins over their eyes — a quiet, solemn ritual that said more about the ferryman's character than any words could. The party collected the masks from the defeated cultists, noting the subtle differences between them, and boarded the ferry as Chris Anthemum finished his preparations. Far away, in a floating observatory somewhere within the Volund Etherium, the Aetherborn stood before a massive suspended model of shifting air currents, watching little markers spring to life and vanish across the map. When a Shattered Will operative rushed in to report that the search team at Outpost Kestrel had been defeated and the Astral Covenant had escaped, the Aetherborn turned slowly, the air in the room shifting in an unsettling way. He said only that eleven viable approaches remained, ordered the search to continue, and turned back to his map — utterly unmoved.
## Rest Aboard the Sky Ferry
Aboard the ferry, the party rested and recovered, finding small bags of peanuts waiting at their seats — a small, thoughtful comfort from their ferryman. Chris Anthemum stood at the helm, constantly checking his wind charts, licking his finger to test the air, and muttering to himself as he navigated the ever-shifting currents. He noted that the winds had been growing stranger every year, with new floating islands constantly reshaping the air around them, though the map had illuminated safe routes through even the most treacherous passages. A small, mysterious creature — quickly dubbed the "sqoud" — appeared from somewhere and cozied up against Vien, seemingly unbothered by the chaos that had just unfolded.
## The Boundary of the Sky and the Cloud Bank
The calm did not last. As the ferry approached its destination, the air currents became visibly strange — looping on one side, smashing together in another — and turbulence began to shake the vessel. Chris Anthemum explained that they were reaching the boundary between where the sky belonged to the people and where it belonged to itself. A rotating ring of thick clouds encircled the destination island, periodically parting to reveal glimpses of it before swallowing the view again. When the ferry finally entered the cloud bank, the fog thickened to an impenetrable pea soup, and the visions began — Krish saw the ruins of a destroyed Dhruvian village, Vien saw  the city of Lux Arcanum before the red pulses had torn it apart, while Quinlan saw his brother, and behind him Fernath, and Sabra saw her mother pressing the pendant into her hands. Chris Anthemum, gripping the helm with white knuckles, began reciting a dark, rhythmic poem in a voice that grew more frantic with every line — faster, faster, faster, there's no earthly way of knowing which direction we are going — as Vien worked to calm the air with soothing magic while Sabra threw her strength into rowing the vessel forward. At last, the ferry broke free of the cloud bank into an eerily calm space, where flowing rings of air and debris — feathers, leaves, and fragments of broken stone — circled the island like a sideways waterfall.
## The Island Harmonic
The island itself was silent and still, with no wildlife and only a single small building of impossibly thin stone waiting at its center. The door slid open on its own as the party approached, revealing a staircase descending into the earth below. At the bottom of roughly twenty steps, the path opened into a breathtaking and unsettling sight — walkways of solid light suspended over a dark River of Shadows far below, connecting small islands of ground like a bridge built from pure luminescence. The party crossed without hesitation, the darkness beneath them yawning wide and silent.
## Wind Harmonic Calibration
Beyond the bridge, a sealed door blocked their path, its surface covered in a technological interface unlike anything simple. The party worked through a careful calibration, identifying wind-related words — gust, aero, storm, zephyr, breeze, squall, and breath — until the final sequence clicked into place and the door slid open. Beyond it lay a massive circular chamber, its floor composed entirely of hardened light that glowed beneath their feet. And standing in the center of it, already waiting, was the Aetherborn — his mask displaying the Roman numeral VI. He greeted them with cold amusement, remarking that they had been considerate enough to bring the harmonics directly to him.
## Confrontation with the Aetherborn and Colton
The battle that followed was fierce and strange. The Aetherborn struck at the party with waves of psychic force, targeting those with the deepest wells of magical energy, while his unnatural scarf floated in a wind that did not exist and absorbed blow after blow. Krish glimpsed the future through a flash of foresight, allowing the party to anticipate and counter the Aetherborn's moves, and Quinlan taunted him with biting insults about his mask and his followers, landing strike after strike infused with light. The Roman numerals on the Aetherborn's mask shifted and counted down as the fight wore on — VI, then V, then IV — each change a silent signal that the end was approaching. Vien unleashed a torrent of fire that scorched through the Aetherborn's defenses, and the party discovered that lightning, above all else, was what he truly feared.

As the final marking on the Aetherborn's mask disappeared, a spear crashed into the center of the platform and Colton stepped out of the shadows. He was visibly worn and scarred from their previous encounters, but his fury was undimmed — and around his neck, he wore Quinlan's stolen acorn pendant. The sight of it sent Quinlan into a rage, and the party turned their focus to Colton even as the Aetherborn began to unravel, strings of light peeling away from him and drifting back up through the ceiling, his magical scarf dissolving with him. Colton fought hard but the tide had already turned, and when Quinlan reclaimed the pendant from him mid-battle, Colton made his choice — zipping away into the darkness with a parting remark and vanishing from the chamber.
## Visions of Dhruv
With the Aetherborn gone and Colton fled, the chamber fell silent. At the center of the room, resting on a plinth, was the Wind Harmonic. The moment the party claimed it, the hardened light beneath their feet disappeared and they floated in open space as a vision unfolded before them — vast Dhruvian forests, their root systems spreading deep and wide beneath the earth, the trees whispering knowledge to one another in a language older than words. A single coin spun slowly in the air before the vision faded and the light bridge returned beneath their feet. The forests were Dhruvian, unmistakably so — and somewhere within them, the next thread of their journey was waiting to be found.
